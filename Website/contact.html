Frequently Asked Questions
STEM Astronomy Club is a student activity originating from STEM 6th of October – High School for Boys. We organize events, discussions, and activities in order to foster a passion for astronomy among students.
The club is open to all students with an interest in astronomy and passed 9th grade (3rd preparatory grade). You don’t have to have great prior knowledge in astronomy. Anyone is welcome to join us.
Just by filling the membership form you are considered for our club.
It usually takes 2-4 weeks after the end of the form’s deadline. Some troubles are often faced within the process of selecting members. These troubles may delay the process of sending decisions.
There are no fees to join the club. The activities we do are totally free to join.
The club meets once a week, typically on Friday. We have discussions on specific astronomy topics and elaborated by organizers.
The club organizes weekly discussions, yearly events, and houses’ (like the ones in the Harry Potter series) activities.
No prior knowledge is required. The club is open to all students with all skill levels, and we aim to provide a welcoming environment for everyone to learn and grow.
